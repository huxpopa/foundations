# Software as a Service (SaaS)

## Build

**Know What You Use:** Maintain a complete list of all approved SaaS applications used in the organization.

**Establish an Approval Process:** Define a clear process for how new SaaS applications are requested, reviewed for security and compliance, and approved.

**Centralize Ownership:** Assign a business owner for each SaaS application who is responsible for the relationship and its budget.

**Define Data Rules:** Set clear guidelines on what kind of company data is allowed to be stored or processed in which SaaS applications.

## Manage

**Regularly Review Your SaaS:** Periodically review the usage, cost, and necessity of each SaaS application with its owner.

**Manage Users:** Implement a process for adding, removing, and auditing user access to all SaaS platforms.

**Monitor for Security:** Keep an eye on the security posture of your SaaS vendors and ensure they meet your company's standards.

**Track Spending:** Keep a close watch on the budget and renewal dates for all your SaaS subscriptions.

## Maturity

- [ ] Do you have a single, up-to-date list of all your SaaS applications?
- [ ] Is there a formal process for approving new SaaS products?
- [ ] Do you know who owns the budget for each SaaS subscription?
- [ ] Do you have a process for removing user access when an employee leaves?

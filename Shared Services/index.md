# Shared Services

## Build

**Identify Shared Functions:** Find the common IT and business support activities that can be centralized to improve efficiency (e.g., Service Desk, Asset Management).

**Define Your Service Offerings:** For each shared service, clearly define what the service does, who can use it, and how to request it.

**Establish Service Ownership:** Assign a clear owner for each service who is responsible for its performance, budget, and improvement.

**Create a Service Catalog:** Build a user-friendly catalog where people can go to find and request all the available shared services.

## Manage

**Measure Performance:** Define and track key metrics (like customer satisfaction or resolution time) for each service.

**Hold Service Review Meetings:** Regularly meet with business stakeholders to ensure the shared services are meeting their needs.

**Focus on Continuous Improvement:** Constantly look for ways to make the services faster, cheaper, and better.

**Communicate Value:** Regularly share how the shared services are helping the business save money and operate more efficiently.

## Maturity

- [ ] Have you identified which IT functions are treated as a shared service?
- [ ] Is there a clear owner for each of your key services?
- [ ] Do you have a central place where users can go to request services?
- [ ] Do you measure customer satisfaction with your services?

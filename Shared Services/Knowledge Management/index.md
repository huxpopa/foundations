# Knowledge Management

## Build

**Identify Your Knowledge:** Figure out what important information your team relies on to do their jobs.

**Choose a Knowledge Base:** Select a single, central place where all of this knowledge will be stored and organized.

**Create Article Templates:** Make simple templates to ensure all knowledge articles have a consistent and easy-to-read format.

**Define a Process:** Document how new knowledge is created, reviewed, published, and eventually archived.

## Manage

**Encourage Contribution:** Make it easy for everyone on the team to suggest new articles or update existing ones.

**Keep It Fresh:** Regularly review your knowledge base to get rid of outdated information and fill in any gaps.

**Promote Its Use:** Constantly remind people to check the knowledge base first before asking a question.

**Measure Its Value:** Track how often the knowledge base is used and which articles are most popular to see what's most helpful.

## Maturity

- [ ] Do you have a central place for storing team knowledge?
- [ ] Is there a process for adding new information to your knowledge base?
- [ ] Do you ever delete or archive old, out-of-date articles?
- [ ] Do team members know where to find the knowledge base?

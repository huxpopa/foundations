# Service Desk

## Build

**Define Your Scope:** Clearly define what kinds of issues the Service Desk handles and what it escalates to other teams.

**Choose Your Channels:** Decide how users can contact the Service Desk (e.g., phone, email, chat, portal).

**Set Up a Ticketing System:** Implement a tool to log, track, and manage every single user request and incident.

**Create Standard Responses:** Write pre-defined answers and solutions for the most common and repetitive user issues.

## Manage

**Track Your Performance:** Monitor key metrics like how quickly you answer the phone, how long it takes to resolve issues, and user satisfaction.

**Focus on First-Contact Resolution:** Empower and train your team to solve as many issues as possible on the very first call or email.

**Identify Common Problems:** Regularly analyze your tickets to find recurring issues and then work on permanent fixes for them.

**Gather Feedback:** Use surveys to ask users how you're doing and what you can do to improve the service.

## Maturity

- [ ] Do you use a ticketing system to track all user issues?
- [ ] Do you measure user satisfaction with the Service Desk?
- [ ] Do you know what your top 10 most common user issues are?
- [ ] Is there a clear process for when the Service Desk needs to escalate an issue to another team?
